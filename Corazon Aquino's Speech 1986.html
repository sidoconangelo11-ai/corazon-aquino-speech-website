The Historic Moment

In 1986, President Corazon Aquino addressed the United States Congress to present the story of the Filipino people's peaceful struggle for democracy. The speech symbolized a renewed partnership between the Philippines and the United States after the restoration of democratic rule.

Click to reveal background of the speech

The address came months after the People Power Revolution, when millions of Filipinos peacefully protested and restored democratic leadership.

Click to reveal why the speech mattered

The speech helped secure international support for rebuilding the Philippine economy and democratic institutions.

Main Themes of the Speech

Democracy

Aquino emphasized that democracy was restored through peaceful civic action rather than violence.

Unity

The revolution united Filipinos from different backgrounds who believed in freedom and justice.

International Cooperation

Aquino asked for support from the United States and the global community to help rebuild the nation.

Impact of the Speech

The address strengthened diplomatic ties and brought global attention to the peaceful democratic movement in the Philippines.
